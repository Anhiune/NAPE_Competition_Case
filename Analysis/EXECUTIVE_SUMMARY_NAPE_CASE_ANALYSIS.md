## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This comprehensive financial analysis evaluates three strategic investment scenarios for an independent power producer (IPP) seeking to capitalize on the AI/data center power demand boom while achieving aggressive growth targets of over 30% annual adjusted free cash flow per share growth.

### Three Strategic Scenarios Analyzed:

1. **Scenario A: Co-located Nuclear Partnership** (Talen-AWS Model)
   - Acquisition of 800 MW existing nuclear capacity with long-term data center PPA
   - Direct partnership model inspired by Talen Energy's $18B, 17-year AWS deal

2. **Scenario B: Build New CCGT Natural Gas**
   - Greenfield construction of 550 MW combined cycle gas turbine
   - Positioned for data center adjacency with flexible, dispatchable generation

3. **Scenario C: Acquire Existing CCGT Natural Gas**
   - Purchase of 550 MW existing gas plant for immediate cash flow
   - Lower upfront capital with shorter remaining economic life

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### RECOMMENDED HYBRID APPROACH

**Primary Strategy: Scenario A (Nuclear Partnership) + Scenario C (Gas Acquisition)**

**Rationale:**
1. **Acquire Existing Nuclear Asset ($3.0B)** with long-term data center PPA
   - Establishes company as "AI power provider" potentially earning higher market multiple
   - Provides stable, contracted baseload generation (6.45 TWh/year)
   - Delivers $392M average annual EBITDA
   - Positions company for future data center deals across portfolio

2. **Simultaneously Acquire Existing CCGT ($450M)** for immediate cash flow
   - Generates $108M annual EBITDA to support growth targets
   - Positive NPV provides balance sheet cushion
   - 4-5 year payback provides near-term cash flow
   - Maintains flexible, dispatchable capacity for PJM market participation

**Combined Financial Impact:**
- Total Capital Required: $3.45B
- Combined Annual EBITDA: ~$500M
- Combined Annual FCF: ~$195M
- Immediate EBITDA accretion supporting 30%+ FCF/share growth target

**Financing Structure:**
- 50% debt ($1.725B) at 8% cost
- 30% equity ($1.035B) at 12% cost
- 20% partner/project financing ($690M) from data center customer
- Maintains BB credit rating with revenue certainty from PPA

---

### CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

1. **Contract Structure with Data Center Partner**
   - Fixed pricing mechanism with floor/ceiling to share upside
   - Minimum volume commitments with penalty payments (Talen-AWS model)
   - 15-20 year term matching nuclear plant economics
   - Front-of-meter arrangement approved by PJM and FERC

2. **PJM Market Position**
   - Maintain wholesale market participation for non-contracted capacity
   - Capture capacity market payments ($150k/MW-year for nuclear, $100k/MW-year for gas)
   - Benefit from Northern Virginia data center hub location premium

3. **Growth Platform Development**
   - Use nuclear partnership as template for expanding data center relationships
   - Position company for potential SMR development opportunities
   - Leverage "clean energy" story for higher market multiple

4. **Risk Management**
   - Hedge natural gas price exposure for CCGT asset
   - Structure carbon cost pass-through mechanisms where possible
   - Maintain operational excellence at nuclear facility (>92% capacity factor)

### SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

**Upside Scenarios:**
- **Carbon price increase to $50/ton:** Gas LCOE increases $18/MWh, nuclear advantage grows to $28/MWh
- **Higher PPA pricing ($70/MWh):** Nuclear NPV becomes positive at ~$300M
- **Multiple expansion:** If market values company at 40x EBITDA (vs 30x), market cap increases $2-4B

**Downside Scenarios:**
- **Lower PPA pricing ($50/MWh):** Nuclear margins compressed but zero-carbon value remains
- **Extended nuclear outage:** Need robust operating reserves and backup capacity
- **FERC regulatory changes:** Front-of-meter structure mitigates co-location regulatory risk

### Methodology Notes

**Financial Model Structure:**
- Discounted cash flow analysis using project-level WACC
- Annual projections with O&M escalation and MACRS depreciation
- Revenue from energy sales + capacity market payments
- Operating costs include fuel, O&M (fixed + variable), and carbon costs
- Free cash flow to firm calculated as operating cash flow less interest expense
- NPV calculated as sum of discounted annual FCF less initial investment

**Limitations:**
- Does not include transmission upgrade costs (typically borne by utility/RTO)
- Assumes PPA pricing remains constant (in reality, may include escalation clauses)
- Nuclear operating costs based on current fleet average (could vary by plant)
- Does not model detailed debt amortization schedule (simplified interest-only approach)
- Market price assumptions based on current PJM conditions (subject to change)
